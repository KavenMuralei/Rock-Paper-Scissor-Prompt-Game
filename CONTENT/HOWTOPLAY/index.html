How To Play

Introduction

When loading the game, you will see an alert welcoming the user, asking the user for their name, prefered mode of Rock, Paper, Scissors, and lastly how many rounds the user desires to play for. An error will occur when the number of rounds is not even, as a winner would not be chosen.

Modes

Classic Mode

Insane Mode

Classic mode is exactly like Rock Paper Scissors but with alerts. In this mode, a tie re-do's a round, meanwhile a win is considered a win, and a loss is a loss

Insane mode is Rock Paper Scissors, but with a twist. A tie and loss both end up with a loss for the player. If the player wins the round, they will need to answer a question. If the player failed to answer, they also lose the round. The questions will mainly be geography and math related conspects.

Intermissions

In our game there are "Intermission" alerts. The premise of having these alerts, was to create a feeling that you are playing rock, paper and scissors, rather than a simple game against the AI.

Classic Mode Rounds

As stated before, Classic mode is the normal Rock, Paper, Scissors game but with alerts. The win alert means you won the round, a lost alert means you lost the round, and lastly the tie alert redo's your turn, to make the game more intense.

Insane Mode Rounds

Insane mode is a gamemode we created, as a different take onto rock, paper, scissors. When a player wins the rock, paper, scissors duel against the AI, the player is forced to answer a question. If they fail do so, they lose the round. Tying against the AI also results in a loss, including the normal loss to the bot.

Win and Lost

After all the round have finished, if the player has more wins than the AI, they get presented with a victory alert, but if they have lost more than wins, they get presented with a defeated alert.
